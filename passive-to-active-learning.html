Turn a Talk into an Active Learning Experience

Why?

Attending a conference talk can expose you to new ideas or technologies, but you are passive during the presentation. In order to gain insight into new concepts, or be able to apply new skills, after the talk you will have to do the work of transforming your notes into an active learning experience. In this blog post, I explain how.

Step 1: Before the talk, set your intention.

In order to Decide what you want to be able to do with the information. More specifically, Do you just want exposure to a topic? Do you want to be able to use a idea, concpet, or technology in a project, or do you want to break something down into it’s component parts? Do you want to create something new, or find deeper patterns and connections? If you are attending several talks at a conference, you won't have time do build or create for every talk, so be picky.

Step 2: During the talk, take notes.

Step 3: After the talk, write your learning objectives using verbs aligned to Bloom's Taxonomy

Do you just want exposure to a topic? Then write objectives using verbs from the comprehension and knowledge tier of Bloom’s Taxonomy. Do you want to be able to use a idea, concpet, or technology in a project, or do you want to break something down into it’s component parts? Use Application or analysis verbs. Do you want to create something new, or find deeper patterns and connections? Use verbs from synthesis and evaluation.

Image from Fractus Learning

Step 4: Do each objective, then 'Name the Steps.'

Step 5: Use your steps to do it again and again until you develop fluency with the skills

See it in Practice

On August 4, 2016, I attended a talk by Ryan Ciecalone called “Node for beginners, Why so Asynchronous?” During the talk, I took notes, and drafted objectives. After the talk, I spent 2+ hours following the process described above. See my Github repo here.
